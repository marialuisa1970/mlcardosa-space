Why connection still beats the algorithm.

María Luisa Cardosa Düsseldorf · 2026 6 min read

We keep asking what AI will replace. I think the more useful question is what it can never touch — and why that is exactly where we should be spending our attention.

I spend my days between two worlds. In one, I coach people through change — a career shift, a return to work, the quiet fear of starting something new at fifty. In the other, I teach those same people how to use artificial intelligence. The two feel like opposites. They are not.

The most common thing I hear, from learners of every age, is some version of: "Will this make me unnecessary?" It is a real fear and it deserves a real answer. So here is mine.

What the tool is good at

A model can draft, summarise, translate, and tirelessly explain the same idea five different ways until one of them lands. That last part matters more than people admit — patience at scale is genuinely new, and for a learner it can be a gift.

But notice what is missing. The model does not know that you cried in the car before the interview. It does not remember that this is your third attempt, or why the second one broke your confidence. It has no stake in whether you actually grow.

AI can accompany you, listen, and think alongside you — but it cannot give you what another person gives you.

Where connection lives

Learning sticks when someone believes in you slightly before you believe in yourself. That belief is not information; it cannot be retrieved or generated. It is built, slowly, in the space between two people who have decided to pay attention to each other.

So I have stopped treating AI as a rival to that work. I treat it as a tool that clears the busywork — the drafts, the repetition, the first ninety percent — so that the human part has more room, not less. The goal was never to automate connection. It was to protect it.
