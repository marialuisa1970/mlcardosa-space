Learning at any age is never too late.

María Luisa Cardosa Düsseldorf · 2026

I used to think learning was something you did when you were young. But life has a way of changing your mind.

School, college, early career. That was the time to grow. After that, it was about doing your job and keeping up.

I've met people who started new careers in their fifties. Learned to code in their sixties. Wrote their first book at seventy. And what they all share is not just courage, but curiosity. They didn't wait for permission. They just began.

It's about attitude, not age

Learning isn't about age. It's about attitude. It's saying I don't know yet. It's being okay with being a beginner again. And the truth is, your brain doesn't stop being able to learn. It just needs a reason to try.

Maybe that reason is a new hobby. A grandchild who asks questions you can't answer. Or just the quiet feeling that you're not done becoming who you are.

Just take the first small step

So if you've been thinking about starting something new — a language, a skill, a course — don't wait for the right time. There may never be one. Just take the first small step. Watch one video. Read one page. Ask one question.

Because it's not about how fast you go. It's about not stopping.

And it's never too late to begin.
