AI in education: a tool, not a teacher.

María Luisa Cardosa Düsseldorf · 2026

When you are an adult learning something new, the challenges are different. You may be tired after work. You might feel self-conscious about being a beginner again. Or you worry you are too old to pick it up.

AI can help. It lets you learn at your own pace, anytime, without judgment. It can explain grammar. It can walk you through a math problem. It can help you practice a new language with no fear of mistakes.

But it cannot replace the human support that adult learners need most.

What we really need

Because what we really need is not just information. It is encouragement. It is someone who says you are not behind. You are not too old. You are doing great. It is a teacher or mentor who understands life gets in the way. Work. Family. Stress. And still believes in your growth.

AI does not notice when you are discouraged. It does not say take a break. You have earned it. It does not remember your goals or cheer when you reach them.

Keep real connection alive

So let us use AI as a helper. For practice. For repetition. For access. But let us keep real connection alive. Because adult learning is not just about skill. It is about courage. Trust. And the feeling of being seen.

And that is something only people can give.
