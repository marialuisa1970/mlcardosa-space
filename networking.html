— 01 / About

Not mentorship.
Exchange.

The most valuable knowledge doesn't flow in one direction. It lives between people, across the table.

What happens when a woman with 30 years of hard-won experience sits down with one just starting out — and both of them are expected to listen? Something that rarely happens in formal mentoring programs.

The Networking Space is built on one belief: that every generation holds something the other needs. Older women carry the texture of experience — decisions made under pressure, the things they'd undo, the clarity that only comes with time. Younger women carry something just as rare: a fresh angle, digital fluency, and the kind of honesty that doesn't yet know it should stay quiet.

We bring them together. Not on a stage. At a table, or on a screen, face to face.

01
Real exchange
Not advice-giving. Mutual listening. Both sides share; both sides leave with something new.
02
Small groups

— 03 / Who's behind it

The person in the room.

María Luisa · Düsseldorf · 2026
Founder · Educator · Connector

María Luisa
Cardosa.

For over 20 years, I've watched generations sit in the same room and still manage to miss each other. The older women holding back the hard-won things they know. The younger ones afraid to ask the questions that actually matter.

I built the Networking Space because that waste is avoidable. I'm an educator and coach based in Düsseldorf, working at the intersection of learning, communication, and what AI can — and can't — do for human connection.
